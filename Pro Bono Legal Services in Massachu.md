Massachusetts Commission Against Discrimination (MCAD) – Enforces anti-discrimination law (housing, employment, public accommodations). If eligible, MCAD provides free intake and investigation for discrimination claims (www.mass.gov/mcad; no fees).



Disability Law Center (DLC) – (see above) helps people with disabilities facing employment discrimination, including ADA access.

Consumer Bankruptcy Project (Boston Bar Assoc.) – Pro bono volunteer attorneys file Chapter 7 for income-eligible debtors (applications via Boston Bar Legal Services Center, tel. 617‑338‑0530).



Greater Boston Legal Services – handles bankruptcy (Chapter 7) for low-income clients

.



Massachusetts Legal Clinic of the Federal Courts – in Spring and Fall, law students give free debt relief sessions.

Sliding-Scale Legal Services in Massachusetts



(Note: “Sliding scale” typically means nonprofit or private attorneys charging reduced fees based on income.)



De Novo Center for Justice and Healing (Cambridge) – Free pro bono intake and counseling for victims of domestic violence and trafficking; offers legal services on a sliding-scale fee basis. Practice areas: housing (evictions, tenant rights), family law (DV-related), SSI/SSDI, immigration (asylum, VAWA), and mediation

harvardlegalaid.org

. Address: 47 Thorndike St, Cambridge, MA 02141

opencounseling.com

. Tel. 617‑661‑1010. Languages: English/Spanish (medical interpreters available).



Berkshire Center for Justice (BCJ) – Regional nonprofit (Great Barrington) offering free legal clinics and sliding-scale fees for civil matters

berkshirecenterforjustice.org

. Programs include “Justice for Elders,” weekly walk-in clinics (e.g. Guthrie Center lunch, Wed 12–1pm), and low-cost family and housing representation

berkshirecenterforjustice.org

. Office: 43 Railroad St, Pittsfield, MA 01201 (tel. 413‑854‑1955). Languages: English (interpreters upon request).



Legal Justice Access, Inc. (Lawrence) – Nonprofit providing representation to low- and moderate-income clients (Essex \& Middlesex counties) via a sliding-scale, income-based fee model

legalaidoffices.com

. Takes family, housing, consumer, and benefits cases that are above Legal Aid income limits. Office: 60 Island St, Ste 105, Lawrence, MA 01840 (tel. 857‑264‑2381)

immigrationadvocates.org

legalaidoffices.com

. Workshops and “Ask A Lawyer” clinics also offered. (No one is turned away for inability to pay.)



Senior Legal Helpline (Referral Service) – As noted above, while not directly providing sliding services, this VLP hotline refers seniors to attorneys who charge on a sliding-scale

mass.gov

. If caller has some ability to pay, the Helpline connects them to reduced-fee lawyer networks.



Massachusetts Bar Association (MBA) Lawyer Referral Service – (not nonprofit, but state bar service) Allows callers to be matched with attorneys in all areas of law; offers reduced-fee referrals in some fields for moderate-income clients

masslawhelp.com

. Contact: (617) 654‑0400 or toll-free 866‑627‑7577. (Initial half-hour consultation fee is reduced for eligible callers

masslawhelp.com

.)



MCLE Referral and Sliding-Fee Panel – The Massachusetts Continuing Legal Education (MCLE) system administers a panel of volunteer attorneys who will represent eligible clients at reduced rates in family law, housing, and other areas. Call MCLE Referral Line at 800‑992‑0808 or visit www.masslegalservices.org/sliding (check local programs).



Law School Clinics (Sliding) – Some law school clinics accept higher-income clients on sliding fees: e.g. UMass Law Clinic (Dartmouth) in family law/housing, or BU Law Low-Income Taxpayer Clinic (Boston). Fees vary.



Selected Private Firms (Sliding-scale): Some small firms advertise sliding fees (e.g. family law or immigration attorneys). Example: Goldstein \& Feuer, Cambridge (immigration law on sliding fee

goldsteinandfeuer.com

), The Law Offices of Gregory Coleman (disability). Note: These are private lawyers; verify credentials and fee structure.
